A Profile · By Peachtree Reverse

Equity, unhurried.

Jamie Whitfield grew up between a tobacco farm outside Macon and a brick bungalow in Virginia-Highland — a Southern childhood, by his own telling, that taught him two things above all others: that homes hold more than walls, and that a family's worth ought never be measured by a single quarter's spreadsheet.

Nineteen years on, those convictions still shape how he practices. As a Senior Reverse Mortgage Advisor with Peachtree Reverse Mortgage Group, Jamie has quietly become one of the Southeast's most sought-after counselors for homeowners aged sixty-two and over — guiding more than nineteen hundred families through HECM lines, jumbo reverse products, and the increasingly elegant world of reverse-for-purchase transactions.

He underwrites through Movement Mortgage — a partnership he chose for its Southeastern footprint, its operational discipline, and, as he likes to put it, "the simple fact that they answer the phone." Together they've funded north of half a billion dollars in reverse transactions for Georgia families who, by and large, are not looking to leave Atlanta — only to live in it more comfortably.

What sets Jamie apart, his clients say, is patience. There are no whiteboards, no scripted pitches. Most first conversations happen over chicory coffee at his Buckhead office, or, if you'd rather, on your own front porch.

— Jamie Whitfield

Dispatch · Atlanta, Georgia

A city of porches, peaches & long memory.

Atlanta is, among other things, a city that refuses to forget. Its bungalows still wear their original wraparound porches; its grandparents still host Sunday supper; its retirees, increasingly, are choosing to stay rather than chase the Florida sun. The reason is rarely complicated — the grandkids are here.

From Buckhead's tree-lined estates to the craftsman homes of Virginia-Highland, the high-rise pied-à-terres of Midtown, and the close-in charm of Decatur, Jamie's clients tend to share a single quiet goal: to age inside the home they raised a family in, without trading dignity for liquidity.

The BeltLine has only accelerated the trend. Homes that once felt suburban now feel central, and equity that once sat dormant has compounded into something genuinely useful. A well-structured reverse mortgage — Jamie will tell you, slowly, over a second cup — can turn that compounding into a monthly check, a line of credit, or simply the peace of knowing the mortgage payment is gone for good.

The Approach

Four tenets, drawn from nineteen years of listening.

"A reverse mortgage isn't a product. It's a conversation about what you want the next twenty years to look like — and then the math to support it."

— Jamie
i.

The family is the client.

Every recommendation is reviewed alongside adult children, trusted advisors, and — where it's wanted — the family's CPA or estate attorney. Nothing is signed in private.

ii.

Equity as a tool, never a temptation.

A HECM line of credit, a tenure payment, a reverse-for-purchase — each instrument is selected to solve a stated problem, not to maximize a draw.

iii.

The home stays the home.

Multigenerational households are increasingly the norm in Atlanta. Structures are built to keep the home in the family — not out of it.

iv.

Plain English. Always.

If a clause cannot be explained over coffee to a grandparent, it has no business being signed by one. Disclosures are read aloud. Questions are welcomed twice.
