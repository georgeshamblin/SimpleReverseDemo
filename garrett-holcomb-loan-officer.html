About

A patient hand for a significant decision.

I'm Garrett Holcomb — born on the Western Slope, schooled in Boulder, and rooted in Denver for the better part of twenty years. I specialize in reverse mortgages for homeowners across the Front Range and into the high country, working with the institutional backing of Cornerstone Home Lending.

My clients are typically 62+, often retirees who moved here for the climate, the mountains, or to be near grandchildren. Many own family cabins that have appreciated quietly for a generation. The question I help answer is rarely can we do this — it's should we, and how do we structure it so it serves the next twenty years, not just the next twenty days.

I move slowly on purpose. There is no template here. Every cabin, every Cherry Creek bungalow, every Washington Park craftsman has its own story — and I'd like to hear yours before we talk numbers.

The City

Denver: thin air, long horizons.

Denver has changed more in the last fifteen years than in the previous fifty. The retiree wave out of California and the Northeast — drawn by the climate, the mountain views, and a slower pace — has reshaped neighborhoods I've worked in since the early 2000s.

I do most of my work along a corridor that runs from LoDo down through Cherry Creek and out to Washington Park — and increasingly into the foothills, where multigenerational cabins above Evergreen, Conifer, and Bailey have appreciated to numbers their original owners never imagined.

Then there's the Denver tech-money retiree wave: founders and early employees who cashed out, bought in Cap Hill or Hilltop, and are now thinking carefully about how to keep that home without draining their portfolio. A reverse mortgage isn't right for every one of them — but for the ones it fits, it can be transformative.

The Approach

Six principles that shape every file.

— 01 —

Institutional Backing

Files run through Cornerstone Home Lending — a multi-state lender with the balance-sheet depth to fund what we underwrite. No broker handoff, no surprises at the table.

— 02 —

Slow on Purpose

Reverse mortgages are not transactions to rush. I budget weeks, not days, for the conversations that matter — with you, with your spouse, with the adult kids if you want them in the room.

— 03 —

Mountain-Property Fluent

Cabins, A-frames, log homes, properties on shared wells, parcels with unconventional access — these complicate ordinary lending. I know which products handle them and which quietly decline.

— 04 —

Multigenerational Lens

Many Colorado families share property across generations. We'll structure the file so heirs, co-owners, and trusts are accounted for — not surprised — from day one.

— 05 —

Transparent Math

You see every line: origination, MIP, servicing, the projected balance at year five, ten, twenty. If a number doesn't make sense to you, we don't move forward.

— 06 —

Here for the Long Run

I close roughly 90 files a year — by design. That cap is what lets me answer my phone three years after closing when something on your servicing statement looks unfamiliar.
